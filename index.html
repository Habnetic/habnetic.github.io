About

Habnetic is an open research lab exploring probabilistic modeling for housing resilience and adaptive habitat design.

The project develops Bayesian frameworks and simulation tools to understand how buildings and urban systems behave under uncertainty — from climate risk to extraplanetary conditions.

All research, code, and data released under Habnetic are open-source, transparent, and reproducible. The goal is to bridge architecture, data science, and statistical reasoning to build more resilient environments — on Earth and beyond.

Research

Habnetic develops Bayesian and stochastic frameworks to study resilience in housing and habitat systems. It draws on open data from scientific and environmental institutions worldwide, integrating them into transparent, reproducible models that help predict and adapt to uncertainty.

The project aims to make complex risk modeling accessible to designers, researchers, and policymakers exploring resilient and adaptive habitats for Earth — and eventually beyond it.
