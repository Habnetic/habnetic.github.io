About

Habnetic is an open research initiative exploring probabilistic models of housing resilience and adaptive habitat design. Developed independently by Mikel Martínez, it bridges architecture, data science, and Bayesian reasoning to understand how built environments respond under uncertainty.

Research

Habnetic develops Bayesian and stochastic frameworks to study resilience in housing and habitat systems. It draws on open data from scientific and environmental institutions worldwide, integrating them into transparent, reproducible models that help predict and adapt to uncertainty.

The project aims to make complex risk modeling accessible to designers, researchers, and policymakers exploring resilient and adaptive habitats for Earth — and eventually beyond it.
